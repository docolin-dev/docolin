# Discussions

!!! info "In one line"
    Every doco has discussions: the place to ask a question, report a snag, or post a fix, and to see whether it's been resolved. Think GitHub issues, but for a guide.

## What they're for

A guide can't anticipate every machine, not even Pango's. When it doesn't quite cover your case, the discussions are where the gaps get filled: questions, caveats, and fixes from people who actually ran it. They're worth a look before you give up on a guide, since the answer is often already there.

## How they work

Each doco carries its own discussion list, opened from the doco itself. A discussion is a thread with a flat list of replies, no nested back-and-forth to lose the plot in, and you can react with a small set of emoji. Reading is open to everyone; posting a thread or a reply needs an account.

## Status tells you where it stands

Every thread is **open**, **closed**, or **resolved**. A fix that genuinely landed reads as resolved instead of getting buried, and you can tell at a glance whether a reported problem still stands.

When a discussion turns up a real fix, it belongs back in the guide itself, and anyone can [propose that edit](./reading-a-doco.md) on the source.
