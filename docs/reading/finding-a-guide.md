# Finding a guide

!!! info "In one line"
    Search from anywhere, and let the results lead with what's proven and what fits your machine.

## Three ways in

!!! cards { cols=3 }
    - **The quick palette**{ icon=command }
      Press **Ctrl + K** (**Cmd + K** on a Mac) anywhere for instant matches as you type; Enter opens the full results.

    - **The homepage**{ icon=house }
      The search box on the front page does the same, straight from the door.

    - **The search page**{ icon=sliders-horizontal }
      The full [search](/search) page, with filters, for the complete set.

## Ranked for you

Results lead with guides that are well [verified](../concepts/verification.md), and then quietly favor the ones written for your setup, nudging them up. You don't configure any of this, and Pango never makes you dig: docolin learns from the guides you read, and that profile [stays on your device](../concepts/privacy.md). A fresh visitor, with nothing learned yet, just gets the well-verified results first.

## Narrow it down

The search page has a facet rail to filter by:

- **Topic**: the [kind](../concepts/kinds.md) tree (`os/linux/firewall`, and so on).
- **Your setup**: the systems a guide applies to, like `ubuntu` or `postgres`.
- **Type**: tutorial, how-to, reference, or explanation.
- **Status and language.**
- **Verified only**, or a **minimum Pango score**, when you only want guides with real proof behind them.

## Or browse by topic

Every [kind](../concepts/kinds.md) is a page. Open a path like `os/linux/firewall` to see everything beneath it, ranked the same way. That browse page is also exactly where a [soft link](../concepts/soft-links.md) drops you when an author links a topic instead of a single guide.
