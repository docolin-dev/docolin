# Licensing and reuse

!!! info "In one line"
    docolin's code is AGPL-licensed and can't be bought or closed off; your writing stays yours, free to read and cite, as long as your name rides along with it.

Pango built his jungle gym so anyone could climb it, and so no one could fence it off and start charging at the gate. docolin works the same way, on two levels: the platform underneath, and the words you write on top.

## The platform is AGPL

docolin's code is licensed under [AGPL-3.0](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.html). Anyone can run it or fork it, but the license has teeth: run a modified copy and you have to share your changes. That is what makes docolin un-acquirable. No one can buy it, take it private, and lock the gate behind them, and it outlives any single maintainer, including the one who started it.

## Your words stay yours

You keep ownership of everything you write. By publishing a doco, you grant docolin and its readers, people and machines alike, the right to display, distribute, and cite your work, with attribution. You are not signing it over; you are letting it be read, on the one condition that your name comes with it.

## An AI keeps the credit, not the copyleft

This is the part that matters most. When a company reads docolin through [MCP](./mcp/connect.md), it does not catch AGPL like a cold; it does not have to open-source its product. What it _must_ do is keep your name on your work, every single time. That is the whole deal, and it is the structural answer to what happened when a big question-and-answer site licensed its contributors' answers to an AI lab: docolin is built so it simply cannot do that to you.

## Mirrored content keeps its own license

Your docos come from your own [git repo](./hosting/overview.md), so they keep whatever license that repo already carries, even docs that were written elsewhere under their own license. docolin reads them and credits them; it does not relicense your work out from under you.
